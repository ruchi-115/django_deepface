What is a Deepfake?

Deepfakes come in many shapes and forms, and can serve different purposes. Most deepfakes are generated using a combination of the following 3 types of actions:

AI-GENERATION

AI-generated images and videos are created by AI models such as Diffusion models (Stable Diffusion, MidJourney, Flux...) or GANs.

They are generated out of the blue or can take a real image as input and modify it.

AI models can also be used to modify parts of an image (inpainting) or extend it (outpainting).

FACE SWAPS

Face swaps are a common form of deepfakes. They consist of taking a real image or video of a person and replacing their face with another person's face.

AI models are used to adapt the angle, lighting, and expressions of the face to match the provided image or video. Results can be extremely convincing.

FACE MANIPULATIONS

Face manipulations are the result of AI algorithms modifying a person's face, either to change its likeness or to change its emotions and lip movements.

This is what so-called lip-sync models use to adapt a person's face to a made-up or modified speech.

Other ways to spot deepfakes

Deepfakes are becoming higher in quality while being easier and cheaper to make. It consequently becomes harder to spot deepfakes with the naked eye. However, at this moment you can still look for several typical graphic inconsistencies (called artefacts) in deepfakes. The typical artefacts to look for are listed below.

Original Image with Localized Face, Cropped Face, Heatmap, and Overlay

Colour Transition

In neural puppetry and faceswap deepfakes, the face is manipulated. Sometimes a hard color transition can be seen on the edges of the area the deepfake software manipulated.

Accessories

Items like glasses might look convincing at first, but when looked closer, might actually contain some artefacts.

Blurry Background or Context

StyleGAN deepfakes are good in making faces, but bad at creating context that makes sense. Check the background and clothing, for example, to see if these make any sense.

Unnatural Looking Eyes

Eyes are rather hard to deepfake due to their complexity. Check if the reflections in the eyes have the same angle. Does the person in the video blink? Are the irises of both eyes equally large?
